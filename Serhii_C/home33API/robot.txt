					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><p>As an Operator of <b>a program automatically consuming the content of the wikis</b> (robot), certain rules apply which depend on the type of content you’re accessing and how you access it. Bots that don't follow these guidelines may be rate-limited and informed of the reason, and pointed to ways to request higher quotas or exemptions.
</p><p>Please note: while the guidelines apply to any bot that connects to our environment, <u>rate limiting is not enforced on bots in Toolforge.</u>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r2344161">.mw-parser-output .note{background-position:left 7px top 50%;padding:0.5em 0.5em 0.5em 40px;margin:0.5em 0;overflow:hidden;background-color:#f8f9fa;color:#333;background-repeat:no-repeat;border:1px solid #ddd}.mw-parser-output .note-inline{display:inline-block;vertical-align:middle}.mw-parser-output .note-info{background-color:#eaecf0;color:#333;background-image:linear-gradient(to left,#eaecf07f,#eaecf07f),url("https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2b/OOjs_UI_icon_information.svg");background-size:25px;border-color:#72777d;padding-left:40px;min-height:25px}.mw-parser-output .note-reminder{background-color:#fff9ea;color:#333;background-image:url("https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a8/OOjs_UI_icon_lightbulb-yellow.svg");background-size:25px;border-color:#fc3;min-height:25px}.mw-parser-output .note-warn{background-color:#fff9ea;color:#333;background-image:url("https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3b/OOjs_UI_icon_alert-warning.svg");background-size:25px;border-color:#fc3;min-height:25px}.mw-parser-output .note-error{background-color:#fee7e6;color:#333;background-image:url("https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bf/OOjs_UI_icon_notice-destructive.svg");background-size:25px;border-color:#c33;min-height:25px}@media screen{html.skin-theme-clientpref-night .mw-parser-output .note{background-color:transparent;color:inherit}}@media screen and (prefers-color-scheme:dark){html.skin-theme-clientpref-os .mw-parser-output .note{background-color:transparent;color:inherit}}</style><div role="note" class="note note-warn">Bots that repeatedly try to get around these guidelines or rate limits, and/or threaten the stability of the sites may be blocked.
</div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r2344161"><div role="note" class="note note-info">The following rules are an evolution of the previous version of this policy which was originally published in 2009. The updates include coverage of more systems, better defined limits, and clarifications to help any Bot Operator who acts in good faith to limit their impact on our systems.
</div>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc">
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="Generally_applicable_rules" data-mw-thread-id="h-Generally_applicable_rules"><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-Generally_applicable_rules"></span>Generally applicable rules<span data-mw-comment-end="h-Generally_applicable_rules"></span></h2></div>
<p>The following rules apply to any activity on our websites; rules in the following sections will be specific to sites/URLs instead.
</p>
<ol><li><b>Consider if dumps are more efficient than live requests.</b> Check if you can use <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Data_dumps" class="extiw" title="meta:Data dumps">Wikimedia Dumps</a> or other forms of offline collection of our data instead of making live requests. If dumps are a viable option for your use case it will reduce the strain on our very limited resources and make your life easier.</li>
<li><b>Accurately identify your User-Agent.</b> Always identify your bot clearly via its User-Agent HTTP header by following the <a href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Policy:Wikimedia_Foundation_User-Agent_Policy" class="extiw" title="foundation:Policy:Wikimedia Foundation User-Agent Policy">Wikimedia Foundation User-Agent Policy</a>.</li>
<li>When you are making a significant number of requests <b>avoid user-agent impersonation</b> and possible collateral damage blocks by either:
<ol><li><b>Provide a URL</b> where we can download a JSON formatted list of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classless_Inter-Domain_Routing" class="extiw" title="w:Classless Inter-Domain Routing">CIDRs</a> from which your requests will originate. See the "<a class="mw-selflink-fragment" href="#What_to_do_if_these_limits_are_too_strict_for_me?">what to do if the limits are too strict for me</a>" section.</li>
<li><b>Authenticate your requests</b> using an on-wiki account when you are making API requests. An <a href="https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/OAuth/Owner-only_consumers" class="extiw" title="mw:OAuth/Owner-only consumers">OAuth 2.0 owner-only consumer</a> is the most ideal authentication method.</li></ol></li>
<li><b>Honor Robots.txt.</b> Honor every directive in our <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robots.txt" class="extiw" title="w:Robots.txt">robots.txt</a> file.</li>
<li><b>Default to gzip.</b> Always request content with an <code>Accept-Encoding: gzip</code> HTTP header to reduce bandwidth usage. This is not necessary when you are directly requesting media files (<i>i.e.</i> images or video) which are already in a compressed format.</li>
<li><b>Respect our HTTP status codes.</b> When we reply with a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_HTTP_status_codes#429" class="extiw" title="w:List of HTTP status codes">429 Too Many Requests</a> status code respect the delay specified by the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_HTTP_header_fields#Response_fields" class="extiw" title="w:List of HTTP header fields">Retry-After</a> header sent with the response.</li>
<li><b>Cached interfaces are preferred and more efficient.</b> If you need the HTML content of pages use either the <code>/wiki/Article_name</code> URL or the corresponding <a href="https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Wikimedia_REST_API" class="extiw" title="mw:Wikimedia REST API">Wikimedia REST API</a> <code>/api/rest_v1/page/html/Article_name</code> endpoint. These requests will be cheaper for us and faster for you because they can be cached in our <a href="/wiki/CDN" title="CDN">content delivery network</a>. More information about these request methods can be found below.</li></ol>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="Website_rules" data-mw-thread-id="h-Website_rules"><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-Website_rules"></span>Website rules<span data-mw-comment-end="h-Website_rules"></span></h2></div>
<dl><dd><i>i.e.</i> https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page</dd></dl>
<ol><li>Always crawl the website via the <code>/wiki/Article_name</code> URLs, with <b>no query parameters</b>. This will ensure that if the content is CDN-cached, you’ll get a faster response allowing you to crawl the site faster and more efficiently.</li>
<li>If you're making read requests, do not emulate a browser - do not store cookies or execute javascript, unless you're not crawling the sites at high volume (so, more than 5 requests per second).</li>
<li>Assuming you are following all of our best practices ideally, still ensure that the maximum concurrent number of requests is fewer than 10 overall, and keep the average requests per second below 20.</li>
<li>Avoid accessing content that is not current, or via non-canonical URLs: do not crawl the site using the <code>oldId</code> or <code>curid</code> parameters, and only use the <code>/wiki/Title</code> format URLs.</li></ol>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="REST_API_rules" data-mw-thread-id="h-REST_API_rules"><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-REST_API_rules"></span>REST API rules<span data-mw-comment-end="h-REST_API_rules"></span></h2></div>
<dl><dd><i>i.e.</i> https://en.wikipedia.org/api/…</dd></dl>
<ol><li>You can use <a class="external text" href="https://wikitech.wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/#/Page%20content">this interface</a> to fetch the HTML content of the pages, or their summary.</li>
<li>Always limit the total concurrent number of requests to less than 5 overall, and the total number of requests per second below 10.</li>
<li>Do not send authentication cookies with your request.</li>
<li>Avoid accessing content that is not current, so avoid requesting a specific revision in your URL.</li></ol>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="Action_API_rules" data-mw-thread-id="h-Action_API_rules"><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-Action_API_rules"></span>Action API rules<span data-mw-comment-end="h-Action_API_rules"></span></h2></div>
<dl><dd><i>i.e.</i> https://en.wikipedia.org/w/api.php?…</dd></dl>
<ol><li>Avoid using the action API for HTML content of pages. Use the website and/or the REST API instead.</li>
<li>Keep the concurrency of your requests to 1 at a time, and below 5 requests per second overall, if unauthenticated.</li>
<li>If authenticated, you can raise the concurrency to 3 overall, and the number of requests per second to 10.</li>
<li>Avoid using expensive API endpoints: if your request takes more than 1 second to serve, please wait 5 seconds before making another request.</li>
<li>Where supported, use batch requests.</li></ol>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="Media_API_rules" data-mw-thread-id="h-Media_API_rules"><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-Media_API_rules"></span>Media API rules<span data-mw-comment-end="h-Media_API_rules"></span></h2></div>
<dl><dd><i>i.e.</i> https://upload.wikimedia.org/…</dd></dl>
<ol><li>Always keep a total concurrency of at most 2, and limit your total download speed to 25 Mbps (as measured over 10 second intervals).</li>
<li>Only use originals or one of our pregenerated thumbnail sizes, which you can fetch <a class="external text" href="https://noc.wikimedia.org/wiki.php?wiki=commonswiki&amp;format=json&amp;var=wgUploadThumbnailRenderMap">here</a> in json format.</li></ol>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="Rules_for_other_resources" data-mw-thread-id="h-Rules_for_other_resources"><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-Rules_for_other_resources"></span>Rules for other resources<span data-mw-comment-end="h-Rules_for_other_resources"></span></h2></div>
<dl><dd><i>i.e.</i> Gerrit, GitLab, Phabricator, and other wikimedia.org services.</dd></dl>
<ol><li>Always keep a total concurrency of at most 1, and use a delay between requests of at least 1 second.</li>
<li>Pause crawling for at least 15 minutes if you receive a 5xx status code.</li></ol>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2 ext-discussiontools-init-section"><h2 id="What_to_do_if_these_limits_are_too_strict_for_me?" data-mw-thread-id="h-What_to_do_if_these_limits_are_too_strict_for_me?"><span id="What_to_do_if_these_limits_are_too_strict_for_me.3F"></span><span data-mw-comment-start="" id="h-What_to_do_if_these_limits_are_too_strict_for_me?"></span>What to do if these limits are too strict for me?<span data-mw-comment-end="h-What_to_do_if_these_limits_are_too_strict_for_me?"></span></h2></div>
<p>These limits are not per-domain but global for all Wikimedia properties, with an exception for community projects of the kind which would be eligible to run in Wikimedia Foundation’s hosted infrastructure (Wikimedia Cloud Services offerings). 
</p><p>Specifically, bots running in Toolforge and in any other Wikimedia Cloud Services offering are explicitly exempted from such limits. We will still reserve the right to temporarily rate-limit or block individual bots that might be compromising the stability of the websites.
</p><p>If you are an external entity and you need a higher volume of requests, please refer to the <a class="external text" href="https://developer.wikimedia.org/use-content/bot-traffic/">High-Volume access page on the Wikimedia Developer Portal</a>.
</p>
